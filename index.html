How to make a Camel

  • Camels are domestic animals.
  • Camels can survive 15 days without water.
  • Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

How to make a Chameleon

  • Unlike other animals, chameleons continue to grow throughout their lives.
  • Chameleons can rapidly change color by adjusting special cells.
  • When a Chameleon is first born they are called hatchlings.

how to make a Cicada

  • Cicadas are herbivores.
  • When they leave their burrow, sometimes cicadas will make chimneys of mud over the exit.
  • Each species of cicada has a different song.

how to make a panda

  • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.
  • Pandas have 6 toes to grasp bamboo.

how to make a Pigeon

  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.

how to make a teddy bear

  • Teddy bears are owned by many children.
  • Teddt bears are fluffy.
  • They aren’t just for children – 40% of teddy sales are for adults, and a quarter of us still has our childhood companion.
